How is ROI calculated?

ROI = (Net Profit / Total Investment) × 100. Net Profit = Final Value − Total Investment (including any additional costs). Annualized ROI adjusts this for the time period using the formula: (1 + ROI)^(1/years) − 1, allowing fair comparison between investments of different lengths.

What is a good ROI?

It depends entirely on the risk involved and asset class. S&P 500 index funds average ≈10% annually over long periods. Real estate typically returns 8–12% including appreciation and rental income. High-risk ventures might need 25%+ to justify the risk. Always compare ROI against similar risk alternatives.

💡 ROI Tips

  • Always include ALL costs when calculating ROI — hidden fees kill returns.
  • Use annualized ROI to compare investments of different time horizons.
  • Factor in taxes on gains — after-tax ROI is what actually matters.
  • Inflation eats into real ROI — use real return (ROI minus inflation rate).
