How to Use This Mortgage Calculator

What is included in a mortgage payment?

A standard mortgage payment includes principal (reducing your loan balance) and interest (the cost of borrowing). If you enable the advanced options, you can also include property tax, homeowner's insurance, and PMI — together known as PITI.

How does a 15-year vs 30-year mortgage compare?

A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but dramatically lower total interest — often saving $100,000 or more on a typical home. A 30-year loan offers lower monthly payments and more financial flexibility, but you pay significantly more over the life of the loan.

What is PMI?

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is typically required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. It protects the lender — not you — and usually costs 0.5%–1.5% of the loan amount annually. PMI can be removed once you reach 20% equity in your home.

💡 Mortgage Tips

  • A 20% down payment eliminates PMI and can save thousands per year.
  • Even 0.5% lower rate saves $20k+ over the life of a $400k loan.
  • Extra principal payments drastically reduce interest paid.
  • Shop at least 3 lenders — rates vary more than most people realize.
  • Your DTI (debt-to-income) ratio matters as much as your credit score.
