Why inflation matters for investors

If your investments return 6% per year and inflation is 3%, your real return is only ≈3%. A savings account earning 1% during 3% inflation means you're actually losing purchasing power every year. Beating inflation is the minimum bar for any investment strategy.

How the Fed targets 2% inflation

The Federal Reserve aims for 2% annual inflation as the "Goldilocks" target — low enough that prices feel stable, but high enough to discourage hoarding cash and encourage investment and spending. When inflation runs hot (like 2022's 8%), the Fed raises interest rates to cool it down.

💡 Inflation Tips

  • Cash sitting idle loses value — even 4% HYSA barely beats 3% inflation.
  • Real estate and stocks historically outpace inflation over long periods.
  • TIPS and I Bonds are government-backed inflation hedges.
  • Salary negotiations should always account for inflation erosion.
